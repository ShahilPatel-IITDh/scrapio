Sandra Hildreth: “The Wild, Wild West”

By
OutdoorPainter.com
-
Share
Facebook
Twitter
Linkedin
Email
Pinterest

Sandra Hildreth, an artist from Saranac Lake, New York, is currently the subject of a solo show at the Adirondack Artists Guild, in Saranac Lake. The paintings are culled from her pieces painted out West.


Hildreth painting Wild Goose Island and St. Mary Lake, in Glacier National Park, Montana.

“Most people fill up photo albums from their travels — I do paintings,” says Hildreth. “I’ve led weeklong hikes for the Adirondack Mountain Club in Glacier and Yellowstone National Parks over the past five years and I always bring oils and/or watercolors with me. I find I get to experience the environment much more intimately when I spend time painting.”


“Going to the Sun Mountain,” by Sandra Hildreth, 2012, oil, 12 x 12 in.

The artist will give a talk on her work on May 16 at 7 p.m. at the gallery. The show closes on May 28. For more information, visit the gallery’s website .
