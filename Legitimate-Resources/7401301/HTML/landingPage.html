Ultimate Buying Guide That Will Help You To Choose The Best Memory Foam Mattress

If you overspend on a mattress you don’t end up using, it can be really bad when you have to return it or send it back because it’s so cumbersome and laborious.

Before diving into the information concerning choosing a great memory foam mattress, you should know a little bit about the history of this type of mattress. This information will help you when you are working to learn more about this type of mattress so that you can purchase one of your own.

A NASA project is responsible for the birth of the memory foam mattress. The medical industry learned about this mattress material and thought that it may be beneficial in preventing bed sores in their bedridden patients.

This type of mattress is unique in that it was able to spread out the pressure of someone’s body weight so that no specific point was getting more pressure than the others. It also responded well to temperature to prevent sweating that can make the skin moist and further compromise its integrity.

When the first memory foam was created, it had a tendency to crack or compress over time, making it a not very durable mattress option. Tempur-Pedic was the first company to take the memory foam mattress concept and work on it to make it a much more durable mattress option. Patients started buying the mattresses and reporting that they would wake up more refreshed and with less back pain than before.

When this type of mattress first hit the market, it was very expensive and certain options are still costly. However, with new manufacturers in the game, this has brought costs down so that more people can purchase this type of mattress for their bed at home. This has also resulted in different types of memory foam and other mattress components, giving consumers far more choices.

With all of the options available today, consumers will find it easier to find a memory foam mattress that is comfortable and able to meet their needs. The key is to take your time and do your research so that when you make a decision, you can be confident that the mattress will be comfortable and that it will last for a long time.

Memory Foam Mattress Density

Density is one of the most important things that you need to explore when you are looking at buying this type of mattress. You want a mattress with about four pounds of density to get a durable mattress that perfectly marries both firmness and softness.

Since each manufacturer measures density a bit differently, you can use the range of three to six pounds to get a comfortable and high-quality mattress.

When you are using density as a measure of firmness or softness, remember that a higher number, so in this case, five or six, will be more firm. If you want a softer mattress, choose a density of three to four .

You also want to consider your sleep position when you are looking for the right density. If you sleep on your side, you want a softer density, so three or four pounds. If you sleep on your stomach or your back, you want a firmer density, so five or six pounds.

Three Additional Quality Markers to Explore

In addition to the density of the mattress, there are three other things you want to look at to ensure that you choose a high-quality mattress. These qualities include:

  • Indentation load deflection: This looks at the various layers and how they play into the comfort of the mattress. You want to find a mattress with an indentation load deflection that ranges from 10 to 16. Most memory foam mattresses on the market fall within this range.
  • Location of the manufacturer: It is best to stick to mattresses that are made in Canada or the United States. This is because the materials are regulated and more likely to be safer.
  • mmHg: This looks at how a mattress reacts against pressure. You want a mattress that works to relieve pressure and this occurs at 32 mmHg or lower. When there is less pressure, you will not have as many pressure points. This means that you can get more comfortable and sleep easier.

Make sure that when you are shopping for your mattress that you look at the ideal density range, as well as these three factors. When you choose a mattress that meets all of these points, you can be confident that it is high in quality, durable and that it will provide optimal comfort.

Look at the Thickness of the Mattress

The thickness of a memory foam mattress will help to determine how comfortable the mattress is and the level of support that you get from the mattress. The average thickness is three to four inches because this type of mattress is already very supportive .

However, if you have a mattress of this type with a lower density, it is a good idea to increase the thickness so that you have adequate support. Support is important because it keeps your body in alignment and helps to prevent you from waking up with problems like back pain or muscle soreness.

Types of Foam Used in Memory Foam Mattresses

Memory foam mattresses have become popular in recent years, but many people are unaware that there are different types of foam to choose from. It is important to pay attention to this because the different types of foam have different benefits .

Consider your sleeping style, your sleep needs and your sleep habits to choose a material that works best for you to keep you comfortable throughout the night.

There are three primary types of foam to choose from, including:

  • Traditional: This type uses the NASA-developed viscoelastic material. This is the most popular type of memory foam mattress and most mattresses of this type are made of traditional memory foam. This type comes in several density options, but it can hold body heat making your bed warmer than you may prefer.
  • Latex: This is another option that you can look into and it is ideal if you want a memory foam mattress that is made out of more natural materials. This is an eco-friendly option and it is least likely to cause problems with allergies. However, some people are allergic to latex and will not be able to use this mattress type if they have this allergy.
  • Gel: This is a newer type of memory foam mattress material that was created to help keep people cool when they sleep. Unlike traditional memory foam mattresses, the gel material allows for better airflow so that heat is unable to accumulate. The level of comfort and support are comparable to a traditional memory foam mattress.

While the traditional type is the most popular and most widely available, many people are now turning to the gel type of mattress because it has an added benefit. This is because the gel type and traditional type offer the same level of support and comfort, but the gel type also helps to keep your body cool as you sleep.

This prevents sweating so that you are comfortable and to prevent sweat from absorbing into your mattress. If a memory foam mattress absorbs enough sweat, it can start to smell and you may not be able to remove the smell.

What Does the CertiPUR-US Label on this Type of Mattress Mean?

If you want to avoid exposure to certain potentially dangerous chemicals in your home, you want to look for a memory foam mattress that carries the CertiPUR-US label. When mattresses have this label, you can also be confident that they have been rigorously tested and that they meet a very strict set of criteria. This means that the mattress is of the highest quality. Mattresses with this label are also:

  • Made without any known ozone depleters
  • The mattress does not contain any PBDE flame retardants
  • The mattress has low volatile organic compound emissions to ensure optimal air quality in your home
  • The mattress is made without any formaldehyde
  • The mattress does not contain any phthalates
  • The mattress is made without any lead, mercury or other types of heavy metals

Terms You Need to Know When You Are Shopping for a Memory Foam Mattress

There are a few terms you want to familiarize yourself with before you head out to shop for your memory foam mattress. Knowing these terms will help you to quickly assess the mattresses that you are considering purchasing. These terms include:

  • Density: This refers to how durable the mattress will be. To determine the overall density of a mattress, you have to weigh a 12-inch cube of it. In general, the densities of this type of mattress range from 2.5 to 4 pounds per foot cubed. Most chiropractors and doctors recommend that you choose a mattress with an average density of 4 pounds.
  • Edge support system: This looks at making the edges of the mattress more supportive by using a stiffer polyurethane foam.
  • Impression load deflection: This looks at the firmness of the mattress. 20 is the firmest and 8 is the softest.
  • Natural latex: This means that the latex component of the mattress uses the Talalay or Dunlop manufacturing process to process the latex which comes from Hevea brasiliensis tree sap.
  • Resilience: This term refers to the mattress’s ability to rebound. When exposed to pressure, the higher the resilience rating means that the rebound potential is better. You want higher ratings because this translates into a more durable foam used in the mattress.
  • Synthetic latex: This type of latex comes from petrochemicals and it is made in a laboratory.
