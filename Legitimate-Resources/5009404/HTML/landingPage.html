How to Clean and Maintain Cast Iron Pans

1/30/2023

0 Comments

Cast iron pans are unrivaled in cooking quality and are potentially eternal, used a lot in the past and making a comeback in today's kitchens.
Cast iron is a material composed of iron-carbon, perfect both for cooking meat and for vegetables and stews because it has the characteristic of perfectly radiating heat and distributing it evenly to food.
New cast iron skillets are pre-seasoned, so all that is required is good maintenance. I often cook in a cast iron pan, I like to cook meat and vegetables, and the cast iron grill pan is exceptional for grilling and obtaining the characteristic lines.

Advantages of cast iron cookware
Why choose cast iron for cooking food? Because it is a practically indestructible and multipurpose material that makes food better without releasing toxic substances.

Why choose cast iron for cooking?
Cast iron pans can be used on the gas stove and in the oven.
Cast iron retains heat well and distributes it evenly, meaning there are no hot or cold spots in the pan. It is very durable and can last forever if we take care of it properly.
You can use it to cook meat, sauteed vegetables, eggs, and bacon, bake Dutch pancakes, and many other dishes, avoiding acidic foods such as tomatoes and lemons as they can remove the protective layer created during the curing process.
A well-seasoned cast iron skillet has a naturally nonstick surface. With each use, the performance will improve more and more. Regular use and proper cleaning of the cast iron pan improves the "seasoning" of the pan (this is a pre-treatment consisting of a thin layer of fat that seals the iron) which makes it non-stick.

HOW TO CLEAN CAST IRON PANS
Click on the blue words for information on the products

PHASE 1 - WASH
Scrape all the food and wash the pot by hand with hot water. You can use a small amount of dish soap, but it's better not to use it. Do not use abrasive sponges or scratching utensils. To combat odors you can use sodium bicarbonate, which in addition to removing odors, has cleaning power.
If necessary, use a suitable food scraper . For very stubborn crusts, cook on low heat for 3-5 minutes, then use the scraper after the pan has cooled.

STEP 2 - DRY
Thoroughly dry the pan with kitchen paper or a cotton cloth.

STEP 3 - LUBRICATION
Pour a light coat of virgin coconut oil on the inside and outside of the pot and rub it with kitchen paper until the surface is shiny and the oil residues are eliminated.
Put the pan back.

Can the cast iron skillet be immersed in water?
Click on the blue words for product information
No! Soaking your cast iron skillet in water is a rust-safe method. If you need to remove sticky or stubborn food particles, use a scrub brush and/or scraper and rinse with warm water. Then dry the pan thoroughly.
Note : If you accidentally leave the pan in water for too long and rust forms, don't worry! With more care, you can remove the rust and continue to use your cast iron cookware.

Can detergent be used to wash cast iron pans?
Contrary to popular belief, it is possible to use a small amount of detergent to clean cast iron pans, preferably a delicate and ecological one without perfumes. However large amounts of detergent can wash the treatment off the pan, but you can easily recondition the pan as needed. I prefer not to use detergent for cleaning cast iron.
Don't worry if, by chance, you have removed some of the "seasoning" from the pan, because it is possible to "recondition" the pan after cleaning. It's not a big deal and it's not difficult, here's the procedure.

































How to "season" or "recondition" the cast iron pan

Click on the blue words for product information
  1. Washing the pan : Wash the pan with warm soapy water and a scrub brush . Cast iron shouldn't normally be washed with soap, but that's fine in this case as the pan is about to be reconditioned.
  2. Rinse and Dry : Rinse and dry the pan thoroughly with paper towels or a cotton dish towel.
  3. Lubrication : Pour 1-2 tablespoons of virgin coconut oil, or extra virgin olive oil, into the pan.
  4. Polish : Use paper towels or a cotton dish towel to polish the pan, both inside and out.
  5. Pan Cooking : Place the pan on the middle shelf of the oven. Place aluminum foil under the shelf to catch any oil drips. Bake for 1 hour in a preheated static oven at 200C.
  6. Let the Pan Cool : Turn off the oven and allow the pan to cool completely before removing it from the oven. When it is cooled it can be stored.
Notes: A seasoned pan is smooth, shiny, and nonstick. If food sticks to the surface or if your pan appears dull or rusty, it's time to recondition your pan.

Can the steel sponge be used to clean the cast iron skillet?
Click on the blue words for product information
No! It is recommended to use a special resistant plastic scraper for cast iron pans and/or the special Scrub Brush to remove any stuck residues.
Don't be afraid to clean your cast iron skillet. The appropriate Scrub Brush and possibly the scraper are the key to comfortable cleaning in the correct way. If your pan has food traces that are difficult to remove, there are no better tools because they are designed specifically for cast iron.

Can the cast iron skillet be put in the dishwasher?
No! Cast iron pots must be washed by hand. The dishwasher will remove the treatment and possibly cause rust.

Vacuum cleaner purchase advice: What to look out for when buying

5/7/2022

0 Comments

Although vacuum cleaners are characterized by a long service life of eight years and more , now and then a new model is simply needed. After all, technology is also making constant progress in the field of vacuum cleaners, so it is worth buying a new device with which you can clean even more effectively and thoroughly. But what should you look out for when buying a vacuum cleaner? Our guide will show you how to find the best Americanvacuum cleaner for your needs.

Check vacuum cleaner recommendations and your own needs.In general, the recommendation is to do some research before you buy . After all, there are a large number of designs from different manufacturers available on the market. It is always useful to take a look at the vacuum cleaner test or test report summaries, but in any case, you should also think about your own needs and local circumstances.
  • Do you live in a house or apartment?
  • Are there carpets or parquet, or both?
  • Should it be a vacuum cleaner with or without a bag?
  • Are there pets in the household?
  • Are there allergy sufferers among the family members?
You should keep all these considerations in mind and clarify them in advance.
Important purchase criteria for vacuum cleaners at a glanceThe following points play an important role when comparing or buying a vacuum cleaner :
  • easy handling
  • suction power
  • dust holding capacity
  • Filter system (HEPA filter for allergy sufferers )
  • Regulation of the suction power
  • engine power
  • Operating noise (volume)
  • radius of action
  • Scope of delivery and special accessories
  • total weight
  • Energy saving
  • Living space or size of the area to be vacuumed regularly
  • Storage and parking assistance
Ease of use and operation is particularly important Easy, comfortable handling is the be-all and end-all with a modern, new vacuum cleaner. After all, what use is the best suction power if vacuuming becomes torture? Therefore, a healthy, back-friendly way of working must be given and an ergonomic handle , with which you can best make all important settings, is a must. The length of the telescopic tube must be adjustable and thus perfectly adaptable to your height.
But smooth-running and gentle rollers as well as the largest possible radius of action - guaranteed by a long cable length and suction hose - are important criteria so that you don't have to constantly change the socket. Of course, the vacuum cleaner must not be too heavy and bulky, so that the device can be carried and easily stowed away if necessary. We recommend that you try the vacuum cleaner directly in the shop so that you can get a better feel for the operation. If you want to buy a vacuum cleaner at a lower price on the Internet, you should also take a look at the customer reviews.
Tip! In addition to the ergonomic and technical features, the accessories are also important. With a new generation vacuum cleaner, various nozzles and brushes for upholstery or joints are standard.
Consider your living conditions and wishes when making a purchase decision, the living space to be cleaned must also be taken into account . For a small apartment, a handy, maneuverable handheld vacuum cleaner that doesn't take up much space is sufficient. For a detached house with different floor coverings, on the other hand, a classic canister vacuum cleaner with a sled is the better decision. These vacuum cleaners are suitable for vacuuming carpets, tiles, or parquet floors, and it is particularly practical if the suction power or wattage can be regulated so that furniture and curtains can also be vacuumed. Whether you are after the vacuum cleaner purchase advice for a model Deciding with or without a bag is a matter of taste, especially since the devices hardly differ, at least in terms of suction power, thanks to the latest technologies.
More watts doesn't mean more power.But don't let yourself be blinded by a high wattage when buying. The wattage is often wrongly equated with the suction power , according to the motto: the higher the wattage, the better the suction power! But this is not the case. Because the new vacuum cleaners guarantee consistently high suction power even with a low wattage of fewer than 1,400 watts and thus ensure lower energy consumption, as repeatedly in the vacuum cleaner testis proven. This protects both the wallet and the environment.

Pay attention to the filter performance and volume.You should also attach particular importance to the filter performance. Vacuumed dust, which is released back into the living room through the exhaust air, not only affects allergy sufferers but also puts a strain on the respiratory tract in general. The housing of the vacuum cleaner must therefore be absolutely tight and ideally designed in such a way that noise is absorbed. This keeps the dust and dirt inside the device and you can look forward to quiet operating noise. If you want to vacuum flexible in terms of time - i.e. sometimes late at night - then low-noise and quiet vacuum cleaners with less than 70 dB are just right, the operating noise of which corresponds approximately to the volume of a conversation.

What can a good vacuum cleaner cost?The price range of vacuum cleaners is just as wide as the selection of different types and manufacturers . Although there are good models for less than 100 euros to buy, if you invest a little more money, you will also get a better vacuum cleaner. Review summaries can provide very helpful information here. In general, it is advisable to choose a well-known brand manufacturer such as Miele vacuum cleaners, Siemens, AEG, Dyson, etc., even if there are cheaper, cheap products from no-name manufacturers.

But on the one hand, the quality, processing, and suction power are simply better and on the other hand, you will probably not find accessories such as vacuum cleaner bags, vacuum cleaner nozzles, or vacuum cleaner brushes so easily for no-name products. In addition, you do not buy a vacuum cleaner every day, but rather the device should provide reliable service for many years. Therefore, when purchasing, the price should not be the priority.
If the budget is limited, there are always special offers to buy or you fall back on a model from the previous series . It should also be borne in mind that buglessvacuum cleaners are more expensive to buy, but are cheaper to maintain because the vacuum cleaner bags are not available and required. In addition, you should always compare the prices in stores with those on the Internet. Buying online is usually cheaper and delivery is often free.

Proper vacuuming: 5 tips for effective vacuuming

4/19/2022

0 Comments

Vacuum cleaning is one of the most important and unpleasant cleaning tasks on your own four walls. The following tips reveal how often you should vacuum, what you should consider when vacuuming properly so that it is most effective and how you can even save energy and money.
Preparation is everything!
Certainly very few people think about it and just start vacuuming. But this is a big mistake because, without proper preparation, vacuuming takes far too long and is even less fun. If you have to vacuum around countless objects, you unnecessarily lose a lot of time and quickly lose interest.
In the worst case, you might knock over a vase or plant while vacuuming and create a lot of dirt, or you might suck up a larger object and damage the vacuum cleaner . To be able to vacuum as effectively and safely as possible, you should make the following preparations:
  • raise all chairs or remove them from the rooms
  • bring all plants and sensitive objects to safety
  • It is best to tie curtains that are too long upwards or place them on a window sill
  • Secure or remove all power or speaker cables appropriately
  • remove larger objects, such as hair ties, bottle caps, tissues, or pet toys from the premises

Regular vacuuming
Some people love vacuuming or see it as a useful workout and people who find vacuuming extremely annoying or even really hate it. Regardless of which group of people you belong to, there is no way around regular vacuuming in your own home. Unless you hire a cleaning lady, but this costs a lot of money!
To ensure that the floors remain reliably clean, you should pick up the vacuum cleaner and clean the apartment at least every 2 to 3 days. The frequency mainly depends on the room or the use of the room. In the kitchen or the living room, there is significantly more dirt than in other rooms. Depending on the intensity of use, you should vacuum every 3 days at the latest.
Hair and other particles land on the bathroom floor every day and these should be removed every 2 to 3 days. House corridor, hallway, and bedroom are used comparatively less and therefore do not have to be vacuumed every second or third day. Upholstered furniture, such as armchairs or couches, can be vacuumed thoroughly once every 2 weeks. If there are pets in the household that lose a lot of hair or feathers, the corresponding rooms usually have to be vacuumed daily.
Proper vacuuming: How to effectively vacuum!
In general, you should only start vacuuming as soon as the respective room is free. If you want to vacuum properly, it also depends on the technology. It is best to start in the corner furthest from the room door and work your way forward.
You should never drive the vacuum cleaner too quickly over an area , otherwise, it cannot suck up all the crumbs, hair, and dust grains. It is best to go over each spot 2 to 3 times so that the vacuum cleaner gets all the dirt. The more intense the dirt, the more time you should take for cleaning it.
In the corners, you should always remove the vacuum cleaner attachment and only vacuum with the vacuum cleaner tube. If you vacuum tiles or laminate, you should always use a brush attachment, otherwise, these areas can be scratched . Most attachments can be used on both hard floors and carpeted floors with no problem. To be on the safe side, you should make sure that the brush ring is always folded out.

Save energy when vacuuming
Vacuum cleaners consume a lot of energy when in operation. Anyone who lives in a large apartment or even in a house needs a vacuum cleaner for many operating hours per month and consumes a lot of electricity. This puts a strain on the environment and your wallet. You can save a lot of energy when vacuuming with the following tips:
  • It is best to use a modern and energy-efficient vacuum cleaner
  • In general, a buglessvacuum cleaner is more ecological because it generates significantly less waste
  • A buglessvacuum saves you a lot of money in the long run
  • Carpets do not always have to be vacuumed, it is often sufficient to simply beat the carpets out
  • many small pieces of furniture, such as shelves or tables, can be cleaned with a simple duster
When can you vacuum at all?
In Germany, there is no special law that prohibits vacuuming on a specific day or at a specific time. In general, all housework may be carried out at room volume at any time. Of course, this only works with a modern and relatively quiet device. If you still have an old and noisy vacuum cleaner, you might want to think about a new, quiet model. In addition, modern vacuum cleaners are much more energy-efficient and cost less.

Cooking UTENSILS Tips

2/13/2022

0 Comments

It is impossible to cook without kitchen utensils : with them, the alchemy of food is carried out. A knife, a pot, and a pan are enough to cook, but in our day we use many more, right? It is worth stopping to observe what kitchen utensils we use : what they are used for, what materials they are made of, what is essential in them, what is worth investing in.
Cutting tools
Indispensable. A chef's knife and a paring knife or peeler are enough to carry out most operations in the kitchen.
The chef's knife has to last for many years and adjust to the hand of each one . And it must be used with pleasure since it will accompany us for many hours of our lives. It is important, therefore, to stop and choose the chef's knife with which one wants to work in the kitchen: if you want it in one piece, how you want the handle, a favorite brand... Of course, make sure it is made of good steel and the right size. suitable.
The peeling knife or lace knife is small and has a sharp point , ideal for peeling and working with agility or in tasks that require good control of what is being done. Interesting that it is from the same brand: many packs of these two basic knives are sold.
From here, choose the extra knife that is going to be used the most: for filleting, boning, ham, bread They all have to be kept sharp . A sharpening steel is enough to remove small scratches from day to day, and a homemade sharpener to keep them in perfect condition from time to time.
pots and pans
You have to have at least two. A good pot for boiling and making large quantities. And a saucepan to heat liquids, sauces, chocolates, or small amounts.
There are so many materials! The most practical are undoubtedly those made of stainless steel . If you prefer lifelong and beautiful, there is no doubt that there is nothing like Le Creuset enameled iron pots. Which by the way has an Outlet where you can find bargains and opportunities.
The classic aluminum ones are not currently recommended, because they release heavy materials that are harmful to the human body.
Pans
Here the range is enormous. A small one is essential for omelets, pancakes, and crepes. And a large one for the rest of the preparations. In a second degree, a grill to grill meat, vegetables, and fish without oil.
Since its appearance, it seems that non-stick pans are the universal ones. However, there is a movement to recover natural pans , which require nothing more than relearning how to cook with them to have them at home for a lifetime and cook naturally.
Among them we find 3 types:
  • Iron pans: made of mineral iron B like the famous French De Buyer pans , or cast iron. You have to cook with them at medium temperatures. And they should be washed by hand, dried immediately, and covered in oil to prevent rust. On the contrary, they ensure that the food extracts all its flavor cooks naturally, and is for life.
  • Stainless steel pans : the ones used by kitchen professionals . They respond very quickly to heat and are cooked over high heat with them. They are very light and durable.

Well, there are several tips so that the pans do not stick that can help you in most cases, but the first thing you have to know is if your pan is non-stick, or even if you should discard it because it could be dangerous.
Is your pan non-stick? First of all you have to know if your pan is non-stick , because if it is not non-stick, it will stick to you if you don't take additional measures.
It's very simple, if your pan does NOT have a non-stick coating, it is NOT non-stick . Any pan, regardless of the brand, model or material, if it does not have a layer of non-stick coating on its surface, it is not non-stick and it will stick to you if you do not take special precautions.
If there were pans without non- stick coating that were non-stick.Who would buy a pan with non-stick coating with the disadvantages of shorter duration and more chemical elements that they contain?
Therefore, it is like that, and you have to choose, if you give a lot of preference to the pan not having a non-stick coating to make it healthier or because you want to enjoy a pan that you know will not scratch and will last for many years. Go ahead and buy a pan without nonstick, but you already know that you have to take extreme precautions so that it does not stick, although it is a matter of experience.
Of course, we are not saying that all non-stick coated pans are harmful to health, because not all non-stick coatings are the same. The "bad" ones are the old Teflon ones that contained PTFE, PFOA, etc. However, now most coatings are already free of these substances and there is no problem.
No option should be ruled out, non-stick pans, such as stainless steel pans , are durable and healthy but stick if extreme caution is not used. Non-stick coated pans don't stick if you use them right, but you know the coating will wear off little by little no matter how much you take care of it. Nothing is better or worse, each system has its advantages , but you have to choose.
Should I Discard My Pan? When it comes to nonstick pans, there are certain signs that tell us that the pan should be thrown out. Normally this is because the non-stick has lost its consistency and is going to come off, which implies that it is going to be transmitted to the food that we will later eat. This is absolutely inadvisable and means you must throw the pan in the trash immediately. If you notice that:
  • There are bubbles in the nonstick or
  • If the nonstick surface is peeling or
  • If there are deep scratches
In that case, discard the pan immediately, because these signs mean that the probability that it will release non-stick particles that will end up in your food increases exponentially.
How To Make Pans Not Stick? Well, these are the tips that work for both nonstick pans and nonstick pans without coating.These recommendations will make your pan non-stick or less sticky:
  • ONLY IN PANS WITH NON-STICK COATING: The hotter they are, the more likely they are to stick, therefore we will try to heat only as much as necessary, trying to avoid unnecessary "peaks", which on the other hand could also spoil the non-stick coating, which usually resists 240C at most. .
  • To minimize the risk of sticking, you have to do as it was always done, before non-stick pans were invented, that is, use more oil, butter...etc., depending on the model and non-stick capacity of the pan, you have to find out How much oil do you normally need?Of course, on pans without a nonstick coating, you'll usually need more.
  • Continuously stir food.
  • THE MOST IMPORTANT: Above all, do not put food in the pan until the pan is hot, that is, until the pan is hot, do not add food.Never put food cold and heat from scratch.
  • Put the food in the hot pan for just 3 seconds and remove it.Then put it back for another 3 seconds and finally put the food in the pan to cook it.In this way, especially with the meat, it acclimatizes to the pan and reduces the possibility of sticking.
  • ONLY IN PANS WITHOUT NON-STICK COATING: For example, in pans that are entirely made of stainless steel or any other that does not have a non-stick coating on the cooking surface, the pan must be very hot beforehand so that it does not stick.Over time, if you have experience, you will know when it is at the exact point, but if at first you do not know, you can do the "water test":
    When the pan is very hot and WITHOUT oil or fat, completely empty, add half a teaspoon of water and watch.If the water stays like a ball of mercury, as well as floating that moves without a trail or trace (an effect similar to when you pour water into a non-stick pan), then that is the point.
  • ONLY IN STAINLESS STEEL PANS WITHOUT NON-STICK COATING: You can pre- season the pan and you will get a natural temporary non-stick coating.
    • Put the skillet over medium-high heat and add a light coating of oil.
    • Spread the oil while it's hot by tilting the pan from side to side.
    • Turn the pan upside down and pour off the excess oil.
    • Sprinkle a layer of table salt across the surface of the pan.
    • With a cloth or kitchen paper, rub for a few seconds as if you were polishing the pan.
    • With a cloth or paper remove the excess pan and salt.
    • The pan is ready to cook.

What is the best material for cookware

The pieces that make up a cookware have unique qualities, which must be known before using them in the kitchen. With them, an ideal combination is created between the ingredients, the preparation and cooking of the food, to create delicious dishes.

Material for kitchen batteries, which one to choose? In the market you will find cookware made of different materials. Next, we will talk about the most used:
Ceramics Ceramic is an ancient material used to make kitchen utensils. Many gastronomic specialists use it to prepare delicious dishes, since it contains organoleptic properties , which give a lot of flavor to the food.
In addition, the utensils made with ceramic exhibit an ecological and healthy option , since it does not give off toxic gases.

Clay It is a material that retains heat for longer . The batteries made with this material are used to serve preparations or bake. With this type of material, utensils that do not damage its enamel should be used, such as Teflon, wood and silicone.

Our blog offers you the best recommendations and tips for the first time you use a frying pan, if you are worried about damaging it, keep reading.
The pans are displayed at the point of sale ready to use, you can simply wash them with a little water to remove any accumulated dust. However, if you want your pans to last longer, to be better preserved and to maintain the properties with which they are manufactured for as long as possible, do not lose sight of these tips to achieve it.
Tips to brand new a frying pan. The Home Advise frying pans that you find on the market are marked with some stickers inside them that give a reference to the model, its type of manufacture or the characteristics of the piece in question, such as the type of kitchen for which the pan or the size is valid.
The first thing we should do when we get home with our new Magefesa frying pan is to remove the adhesives before putting the piece on the heat source. These Pans are easy to remove without the use of knives or sharp objects that can scratch surfaces and mar the bottom of the pan. If there are adhesive residues, these can be removed with a cloth or paper soaked in alcohol.
Once the stickers have been removed, we take the pan to the sink where we wash the piece with warm water, neutral dishwashing soap and a sponge that does not scratch the bottom of the piece.
We can then proceed to the "curing" of the pan . This is putting the pan (grills or casseroles) on the fire at a minimum degree of heat for a couple of minutes with a little oil in the bottom. Once hot, remove and let cool. Once the piece is cold, discard the oil and wash again in lukewarm water with a neutral soap. Then rinse with plenty of lukewarm water and dry immediately with a soft cloth to prevent lime stains from forming.
The best ready-to-use pans Once this process is done, you will have a pan ready to cook any food. Please note that non-stick pans will prevent food from sticking to them. Avoid placing anything on top of them, or washing them with highly corrosive chemical products to avoid damaging the non-stick layer.
