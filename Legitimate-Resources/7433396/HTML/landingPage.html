How Do Vacuum Cleaners Work?

Vacuum cleaners use the principles of air pressure to function. An easy example to understand this is by using the idea of a straw. When a person sucks on a straw, it creates negative pressure between the straw and the glass it is in.

This forces the liquid to be sucked upward and into the straw. This negative pressure principle is used in vacuum cleaners. Read Here: Best Runner Rugs [Buying Guide]

Furthermore, vacuum cleaners have built-in fans. These fans are electronically powered and rotate at high velocities to continuously create a negative pressure, which sucks up not only dust but other particles too.

If other particles are bigger like a peanut or even a cigarette bud, there is a chance they might get stuck in the filter, but with increased power, even these types of particles can be sucked up.

The suction hose attached at the end of the pipe is designed in a way that more suction is created. Essentially, the power of the vacuum cleaner is not as significant as the narrowness of the hose.

The narrower and more closed the pipe is, the more air pressure is created. Thats why most cleaners have narrow pipes attached to suck up the particles with high intensity.

In order to ensure that the air pressure is continuous, vacuum cleaners have built-in exhaust ports, which throw air outside to maintain the balance. Otherwise, the vacuum created would be short-lived.

Most vacuums come with a filter. As mentioned before, the air is also exiting through an exhaust, which would mean that the dust particles would enter the room again.

The filter catches all particles invisible to the naked eye. It naturally has to be cleaned after a certain period of time. This is an essential feature to ensure that environments in which a vacuum cleaner functions remain clean.

Types of Vacuum Cleaners

Handheld

Handheld vacuum cleaners, as the name would suggest, are based on the idea of holding the vacuum cleaner with one hand while cleaning.

This type is used to clean areas in and around tight areas, but it might not be the best solution for cleaning floors. Handhelds can best be utilized when it comes to portable usage such as vacuuming a car interior.

Canister

Canister vacuum cleaners are one of the most expensive types due to their technological advancements and multi-functional designs. They are a hybrid between the standard upright models and the stick models.

They are powerful machines and feature a slender frame. Due to these qualities, they can be effectively used on both carpets and hard floors.

Upright

Upright models are perhaps the most popular and common types of vacuum cleaners available in the market today. Most advertisements and even entertainment content show upright models.

These vacuum cleaners do not cost too much but are a very powerful tool for your household. They can be used to easily and effectively clean both carpets and hard flooring surfaces.

The upright model also includes multiple features that are easy to use and understand, thereby making it one of the most popular vacuum cleaners.

Stick

The stick models are slender in design. They feature a long stick-type handle and a sleek design. This helps the stick model get into narrow, tight places and is great for accessing those tricky areas where dust accumulates.

They are not exactly the most powerful vacuum cleaners, but because of their design, they do a fantastic job when it comes to cleaning carpets and hard flooring.

Robotic

Robotic vacuum cleaners have recently gained a lot of attention and popularity in the market. They are fancy gadgets that make the lives of the owner much easier.

They do not require any sort of overlooking and managing. Rather, they freely roam around the house, sucking up dust and other small particles.

Another added benefit is that they can easily access unreachable areas, such as under the couch.

Although they come at a very steep price, the robotic vacuum cleaners are the way forward toward an automated household.

Type of Flooring

Carpet

If the house consists of wall-to-wall carpeting, the ideal vacuum cleaner to purchase would be the upright cleaner. As mentioned above, this type of cleaner is a powerful tool and is perfect for cleaning carpets.

Upright vacuum cleaners will also not scratch the surface of the carpets or result in any sort of damage.

Nowadays, these cleaners are also coming with additional add-ons such as dirt sensors, which can help gauge if there are any dust particles remaining that can be scooped up simultaneously.

Hard Flooring

Hard flooring poses different kinds of complications when choosing a vacuum cleaner. The best cleaners to use in this regard would be the canister vacuums.

Canisters are a hybrid and, therefore, would also work best for light carpeting. Normally, a powerful tool is required, but more than power, functionality plays a major role in this regard.

The vacuum should not be counter-productive by scattering the remaining dust particles in and around the vacuum. Fortunately, canister cleaners come with features that allow for a complete cleaning radius.

Furthermore, there are additional features that ensure that hard flooring does not get damaged by marks or swirls while cleaning. They also come equipped with bare-floor brushes to bring added benefits.

All this while also being able to clean carpets makes the canister vacuum the perfect solution for hard flooring.

Type of Cleaner

Bagged

Bagged vacuum cleaners consist of, as the name suggests, replaceable bags that carry the dust particles and store them. Companies can guarantee storage of 0.3 to 0.5 micrometers of dust particles.

Some families may be sensitive to dust, so for them, the best option would be to go for a bagged vacuum cleaner. Although the bag may require to be replaced regularly, it is readily available in supermarkets and changing it wont be a hassle.

Bagless

Bagless vacuum cleaners are more environmentally friendly. They do not require recyclable bags or frequent replacing. Rather, they simply have a container that will show the level of dust accumulated.

They both possess the same physical qualities, but these minor differences can influence the buying decision.

Why You Should Choose This Type of Vacuum Cleaner

Choosing the right vacuum cleaner for your home is important to ensure that the household demands are met.

The choice of cleaner depends on the interior design of the household such as the presence of carpets, hard flooring or a mix of both.

Certain hygienic conditions that the members of the household may have can dictate between the bag and bagless options.

Both options have been described above with reasons for opting for them, which will help you decide which type is best suited for your needs.

If someone is looking for a more environmentally friendly product, the bagless option is suitable as it only requires cleaning the filter rather than replacing the bags and buying new ones from the store.

On the other hand, the bagged option gives more convenience as you only have to change the bags instead of cleaning the cleaner.

Keys to Choosing the Ideal Vacuum Cleaner

Apart from the other features mentioned in this post, there are a few key elements to consider before you make a purchase. Most vacuum cleaners come with additional tools which can be bought or are included in the package, which you must consider:

Floor Cleaning Tools

Most vacuum cleaners come with built-in standard cleaning tools like the basic suction tool. Additionally, one can purchase spinning brush bars or turbo brushes.

Both these tools allow you to remove pet hair from tight areas such as carpets. They create more power and can be easily brushed off as well.

Extra Mini Tools

Such tools include turbo brushes, flexible tools, mattress tools, and extensions. Turbo tools are used to pull pet hair and linen from carpets. Flexible add-ons allow the head of the vacuum cleaner to be adjustable, therefore allowing cleaning to be done on top of shelves and doors.

Mattress tools are special tools that allow for the cleaning of mattresses. This will ensure that any remaining dust particles which are unseen by the naked eye are also cleaned.

Finally, extensions allow for a greater reach, meaning that hard-to-reach or larger areas such as staircases can easily be cleaned as well.

Variable Suction

Variable suction is an important thing to consider when there are multiple floor levels and variations in carpet thickness. This feature allows for adjustment in the dial and suction to be able to cope with the variations in flooring.

Some cheaper models include vents in the suction pipes which can also be adjusted.

Filters

Filters are an essential part of a vacuum and directly result in a healthier environment in your house. Some vacuums have filters that are washable, some are self-cleaning, and some require periodic replacement.

Either way, make sure that the filters live up to their promise of holding more than 99% dust inside rather than throwing them back into the room.

Weight

Standard vacuum cleaners weigh between 2 kg and 10 kg. It is important to consider which weight category best suits your needs by determining what kind of usage there will be.

If there is heavy lifting up and down the stairs, for example, opt for a lighter model. Similarly, if there are people in the house with certain back problems, opting for a lighter vacuum cleaner will be best.

Noise

Vacuum cleaners are infamous for the sounds they make. Some create a very loud screeching noise while running, which can be a real nuisance over time.

On the other hand, there are very quiet vacuum cleaners too, which hardly make any noise. The obvious choice is to go for a less noisy cleaner as no one likes noise pollution, especially inside the house.

Push & Pull Force

Pushing and pulling are different than the weight of the cleaner. This factor is best understood by the force required to move the floor head on different surfaces. Some vacuum cleaners can be really strenuous when it comes to pushing or pulling them over thick carpets.

Reach

It is imperative to always opt for vacuum cleaners which have a larger reach. The cord normally ranges between 6 and 15 meters long. It is ideal to go for the longest if your budget permits.

This way, there is no need to worry about finding sockets and extensions all over the house. The wire harness itself can stretch easily, making it hassle-free to clean.

Capacity

Capacity in terms of vacuum cleaners is considered as the amount of dust or particles that one bag or storage container can hold. This varies between 0.5 kg to more than 4 kg.

If there is a larger space to cover with the vacuum, it is always ideal to opt for one with more capacity. However, if you have a smaller space to clean like an apartment, choosing a smaller capacity cleaner is also a fine choice.

Things to Consider When Purchasing a Vacuum Cleaner

While testing the different models before the purchase of a vacuum cleaner, there are a few tips to be used which can help guide the purchasing process and make it easier.

One thing to consider is to turn on the vacuum cleaner if the store allows and check how noisy it is. Some product descriptions can be deceiving, so it is best to check before going home and having to scream across rooms. Secondly, try lifting it.

This will give you an exact idea of how easy it will be to maneuver throughout the house and if it is a viable option. Moreover, try to lay it flat on the ground and see if it can actually reach tight spaces, like under the furniture, and if it is performing oddly.

In some cases, the vacuum cleaner can become faulty when lying flat due to the movement. Hence, it is advisable to check this before purchase since moving the furniture around every time you vacuum can be a tiring process.

Finally, after understanding the basic controls on the buttons, always get the first-hand tutorial from the salesperson on how and when to change the filters or bags, depending on the model.

This will give you an idea about the maintenance of the product and how easy or difficult it is. Following these steps before making a purchase will also help you shortlist the final product that can best suit your needs.

Tips for Maintaining Your Vacuum Cleaner

A vacuum cleaner, just like any other machine, requires due maintenance over a specific period of time. Some components need replacing earlier, while others can go a bit longer, but it has to be done regardless.

Vacuum cleaners on average run for about an hour daily. They are directly involved in collecting dust from the house, which in itself is a damaging component. Therefore, it is crucial to regularly maintain your cleaner. Here are some ways on how it can be done:

Changing the Bags

Of course, the main component which needs constant replacing or washing, depending on the model, is the bag. It is a common misconception to replace the bags when they are full.

However, this will cause excess dust to leave and settle on the floor or carpet again. Also, if the bags are filled to the full capacity, the suction ability of the vacuum will reduce and it will give a less efficient output.

This is because some particles are bigger than dust, so if they do not find space to go in, they stay out in the form of debris, leaving your effort wasted.

Ideally, the bag should be changed when it is 2/3rd full and not fully packed. A smart way to make this process easier would be to purchase a see-through container, which can be monitored easily with a glance rather than waiting for the vacuum to slow down or seeing dust remain on the floor.

Clean the Brush Roll

The brush roll is probably the second most hard-working part in a vacuum cleaner. Its function is to ensure that the flow of the vacuum is continuous.

However, regularly, small debris like hair or linen might get stuck around the brush rolls, making rotation difficult. This will then cause the machine to not pick up more hair or other such debris.

There are two ways to clean a brush roll: by removing it or while it is stationary. Removing it before cleaning it is a much better option as it allows for proper purification.

It can be cleaned using soap and water or with a wet cloth. Attaching the brush roll properly back in place is also crucial.
