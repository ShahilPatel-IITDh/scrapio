cenchempics :

CARPETED This scanning electron microscope image shows vertical polythiophene nanofiber arrays grown on a metal substrate. By harnessing an electropolymerization process to produce aligned arrays of polymer nanofibers, researchers from Georgia Tech have developed a material that can conduct heat 20 times better than the original polymer. Such materials could be used to draw heat away from electronic devices in servers, automobiles, high-brightness LEDs, and certain mobile devices.

Credit: Nature Nanotechnology 2014, 10.1038/nnano.2014.44
