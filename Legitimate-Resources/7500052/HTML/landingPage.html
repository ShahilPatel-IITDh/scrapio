Apple to take a bite out of the cashless economy

Business
Nissan Tzur May 25, 2018

Technology giant Apple will expand its digital wallet service to Poland, giving its customers the possibility of carrying out cashless transactions with Apple devices.

Apple CEO Tim Cook said the company had plans to launch the ‘Apple Pay’ service in several countries – including Poland, Ukraine and Norway – in the coming months, though he did not specify a date.

This would make the digital payment service now available in 30 countries around the world, 19 of them in Europe – a couple of weeks ago the company launched the service in Brazil.

At a conference to announce the expansion, Cook noted that the number of users of the service had doubled in the previous year, while the value of transactions had increased threefold.

One of the main reasons for the increase in revenue from the digital payment service, he said, was its popularity in public transportation systems in some of the largest cities in the world, including Beijing, Shanghai and Tokyo.

"Apple Pay is now the most successful mobile transit payment system in Toyko," said Cook. With the public transport system now operating in Beijing, the company could expect a significant increase in the company's revenues in the coming months.

Economists and technology experts have long foreseen a move towards a cashless economy, and Apple Pay is the lastest product available on the market. Other forms of cashless payment available in Poland include the BLIK banking service, which some banking apps help facilitate.

Apple’s CEO revealed at the press conference that company had recorded a 16 percent increase in revenue in the second quarter of 2018, to $ 61.1 billion.
