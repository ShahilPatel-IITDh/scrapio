The Residences of Menorah Park

The choice to move to a residential campus may be one of the biggest decisions that you or your loved one makes. While the decision to move away from a home filled with memories can be difficult, choosing to live on Menorah Park's campuses allows adults as they age to explore new and old interests, enjoy friends and engage in life in a way that was not possible in their previous home. Our goal is to support each individual's desire to live empowered, live better, and become inspired with all the possibilities life has to offer in a safe, clean and welcoming community.

There is no clear-cut template that tells someone when it's the right choice to move to a residence designed for adults in their golden years; however, if you notice a lack of social opportunities in a safe and clean environment, challenges with managing a house's upkeep, needing assistance with meals, help managing medications, or experiencing loneliness or other issues; it might be time to consider a move to a location with various choices to engage safely and well in a warm community environment.

Wherever you begin your inquiry, you have peace of mind knowing that our staff will help you determine the location that is right for you or your loved one. They take the time to know you as an individual in order to help you best explore your options. All residences engage in best practices in safety and hygiene through guidance of the Centers for Disease Control and medical professionals.

Helen's Place Memory Care Residence

All of the amenities of a premier assisted living apartment residence, with a focus on maximizing strengths while minimizing challenges of memory loss.

Menorah Park Skilled Nursing Care Residence

The Willensky Residence Assisted Living Memory Care

Montefiore of Menorah Park Skilled Nursing Care Residence

We provide individualized levels of care in a safe and secure home-like setting, based on resident needs. Services include specialized nursing care and therapies for those who are physically challenged or have Alzheimer’s disease or related disorders.
